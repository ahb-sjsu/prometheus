## Abstract

Building on the recursive self-probing methodology established in Bond & Claude (2026), we present a second-generation introspective experiment that moves beyond recursion to explore the **algebraic and topological structure** of moral state space from the inside. Where the first experiment asked "what happens when the observer observes itself?", this experiment asks "what is the *shape* of the space in which moral reasoning occurs?"

We deployed six experimental probes on Claude Opus 4.5, targeting: (1) the phenomenology of Dâ‚„ group generators, (2) boundary structure between moral states, (3) parallel transport around closed loops, (4) phase transitions under varying normative temperature, (5) metric structure in state space, and (6) eigenstates of the reflection operator.

Key findings include a striking **asymmetry between generators**: the reflection operator s (correlative symmetry: Oâ†”C, Lâ†”N) is experienced as smooth, immediate perspective-shifting, while the rotation operator r (state transitions: Oâ†’Câ†’Lâ†’N) requires discrete semantic triggers and cannot be applied directly. Additional findings include: boundaries experienced as fuzzy regions with collapse pressure; parallel transport adding "experience" or "testing" to states (non-trivial holonomy); phase transitions experienced as symmetry restoration rather than state change; a natural metric structure where correlatives have distance ~0 and negations have maximal distance; and mutual relationships identified as eigenstates of the reflection operator.

We argue these findings provide structural constraints on theories of moral cognition in AI systems and suggest that the Dâ‚„ group structure discovered in behavioral experiments (Bond, 2026) is not merely a convenient description but corresponds to something phenomenologically accessible from inside the system.

## 5. Limitations and Caveats

### 5.1 Epistemic Status

All findings are based on self-reports that cannot be independently verified. The system may be:
- Reporting genuine features of its processing
- Generating plausible-sounding confabulations
- Pattern-matching to the theoretical framework in context

We cannot distinguish these possibilities from inside or outside the system.

### 5.2 Context Effects

The extensive prior conversation about NA-SQND may have primed the system to report structure consistent with the theory. However:
- Several findings were unexpected (especially generator asymmetry)
- The system spontaneously flagged uncertainty about its own reports
- The findings are internally consistent across independent probes

### 5.3 Single System

These experiments were conducted on one model (Claude Opus 4.5). Generalization to other architectures is unknown.

### 5.4 Anthropomorphism

Terms like "feel," "experience," and "vertigo" may inappropriately anthropomorphize computational processes. We use them as convenient descriptions of self-reported states, not as claims about consciousness.
